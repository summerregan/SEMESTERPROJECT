Reading guide

This written section gives the academic backbone of the website: the research question, supporting ideas, method, findings, and the larger question of how data changes creativity.

Introduction

Advertising today looks completely different than it did even a decade ago. What used to rely on big budgets, mass media, and creative instinct is now shaped by real time data, platform analytics, and much more targeted decision making. My project looks at how data analytics has transformed modern advertising, specifically on platforms like Instagram and TikTok, where brands are constantly tracking impressions, clicks, engagement, and conversions to figure out what actually works.

This project asks: How has the rise of data analytics transformed decision making and effectiveness in modern digital advertising? I'm focusing on how advertising has shifted from being mostly intuition based to something much more measurable, flexible, and performance driven. I also look at how this affects not just brands, but also everyday users, since what we see online is heavily shaped by algorithms, targeting, and data backed content strategies. My website presents all of this in a visual, interactive way so it feels easier to understand and not overly technical.

Literature review

Research shows that digital advertising has become much more data driven because marketers now have access to instant feedback and detailed insights about their audience. Traditional advertising relied on broad reach like TV commercials, print ads, and billboards, and it was hard to actually know how people responded. Now, platforms let advertisers track things like views, clicks, engagement, conversions, and even watch time. Because of this, success is no longer just about creativity or visibility, but about measurable performance.

A lot of research also emphasizes that analytics helps marketers better understand audience behavior. Data shows what content people actually engage with, what messaging leads to conversions, and which audiences are most likely to respond. On platforms like Instagram and TikTok, this impacts everything from captions to posting times to paid targeting. Another big takeaway is that analytics has made advertising more accessible. Smaller brands and creators now have access to tools that used to be limited to big companies, which allows them to compete in the same space.

At the same time, there are some downsides. While data makes marketing more efficient, it can also push brands to focus too much on performance instead of creativity. Some research points out that when marketers rely too heavily on metrics, content can start to feel repetitive or overly optimized for algorithms. There's also a growing conversation around AI and how it's pushing this even further through automation and predictive targeting.

Methodology

For this project, I used a qualitative and interpretive approach supported by examples from digital marketing platforms, campaign structures, and performance metrics. Instead of doing a full data analysis project, I focused on explaining and visualizing how analytics shape modern advertising. I started by defining my research question and then gathering sources related to digital marketing, social media strategy, advertising metrics, and AI.

After collecting my research, I organized everything into sections. One focuses on how advertising has evolved from traditional media to digital platforms. Another explains key metrics like impressions, CTR, engagement rate, and conversions. The final section looks at AI and the future of paid media, including automated targeting and optimization tools.

I built the final project using HTML and CSS to create a multi page website. I wanted the design to feel clean, elevated, and easy to navigate so the information didn't feel overwhelming. I also chose to focus on Instagram and TikTok because they are two of the clearest examples of how analytics influence both content and paid media decisions today.

I also used AI tools throughout the process, mostly to help refine wording, organize ideas, and troubleshoot coding issues. I didn't rely on AI to do the thinking for me, but more as a support tool. It actually made me more aware of how important it is to still have your own voice and judgment, especially when deciding what ideas actually make sense for your project.

Analysis and findings

One of the biggest takeaways from this project is how much analytics has changed decision making in advertising. Instead of relying on instinct, brands now work within systems that give immediate feedback. Metrics like impressions, CTR, engagement rate, and conversions are essential for understanding performance. They show not just if people saw something, but if they actually interacted with it.

Another major finding is that digital advertising is way more accessible than it used to be. Traditional advertising required huge budgets and mass media placements, which made it hard for smaller brands to compete. Now, platforms like Instagram and TikTok allow businesses to run targeted campaigns with much more flexibility. This has opened the door for more brands to experiment, test, and optimize in real time.

At the same time, data doesn't replace creativity. It just changes how it works. Creative decisions are still really important, but now they are guided by performance data. Content is constantly being adjusted based on what performs best, what keeps attention, and what drives action. This makes advertising more responsive, but it can also make content feel repetitive if brands rely too heavily on what "works."

Another key takeaway is how much AI is accelerating all of this. Automated tools, predictive targeting, and optimization systems are making advertising even more data driven. It seems like the future of advertising will depend on balancing human creativity with machine driven insights.

Conclusion

Overall, this project shows that data analytics has completely changed modern advertising by making it more measurable, targeted, and adaptable. It has changed how brands make decisions, how campaigns are evaluated, and even how audiences experience content online. While analytics has made advertising more efficient and accessible, it has also introduced new challenges around over optimization and relying too heavily on data.

This project contributes to the conversation by presenting these ideas in a way that feels more approachable and easier to understand. Instead of making it overly technical, the goal is to show how advertising actually works today in a way that people can connect to. It also raises some bigger questions about the future, especially with AI becoming more involved. As advertising becomes more data driven, what happens to originality? And how much should brands rely on performance data before creativity starts to feel limited? These are the kinds of questions that make this topic really relevant right now.
